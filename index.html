Why A Pseudonym?

As we evolve in a technology driven age with an estimated 44 zettabytes* of data at the dawn of 2020, there is an expectation that the amount of data generated each day will reach 463 exabytes (per day!) globally. Google, Facebook, Microsoft, and Amazon store at least 1,200 petabytes of information. It has become increasingly necessary for each person to protect their personal data. As a Data Analyst/Data Scientist, I use a pseudonym to protect my data in a world that most people do not realize that we already live in.

*(A zettabyte is 1,000 bytes to the seventh power. In other words, one zettabyte has 21 zeros. There were 40 times more data present in the digital realm at the beginning of 2020 than observable stars in the universe. )
